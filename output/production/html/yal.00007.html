To the year 1889.

Have I no ^weapon‑word for thee—some message brief  
 and fierce? Have I fought out ^and done indeed the battle? wholly? is  
 there no shot left, For all thy affectations, lisps, scorns, ^manifold silliness? Nor for myself—my own rebellious self [illegible] in thee? # two leads Down, down, proud gorge—tho' choking thee, Thy bearded throat and high-borne forehead to the  
 gutter, Crouch low thy neck to eleemosynary gifts. Walt Whitman   [begin leaf 1 verso]
