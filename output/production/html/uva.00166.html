Paumanok.

Sea-beauty! stretch'd and basking! One side thy inland ocean laving, broad,
with copious commerce, steamers, sails, And one the Atlantic's wind caressing, fierce
or gentle —and —with mighty hulls dark-gliding
in the distance: Isle of sweet drinking-water—healthy air
and soil!—isle of the earth and brine! Walt Whitman sent to Herald Feb. 27 '88   [begin leaf 1 verso]
